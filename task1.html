Task Assigned

I was responsible for configuring conversion tracking on GA4 using Google Tag Manager. To ensure conversions from different marketing sources were tracked accurately, I had to apply simple coding logic and session storage, particularly because the website contained dynamic content such as different courses and their respective fees. This required capturing variable values correctly so that each conversion reflected the specific course and cost selected. This task also required technical precision which was challenging for me to get a hang of at the start. I applied concepts similar to what we learnt in AI & Robotics, such as logical sequencing and basic coding structures to configure tags and triggers accurately with the help of Gen AI to refine and troubleshoot my implementation.

Turning Point

A key turning point happened when a configuration mistake I made affected the live website. That impacted my confidence and made me realise that digital implementation carries real operational consequences.
I learnt the importance of testing carefully before publishing changes. Instead of adjusting multiple elements at once, I began isolating variables to identify the exact source of errors. This structured troubleshooting approach allowed me to debug more effectively and reduced the risk of repeated mistakes. I also recognised that technical development often involves multiple rounds of trial and error before achieving a stable and functional outcome.
After multiple rounds of testing, debugging, watching tutorials, and troubleshooting using documentation and GPT, I successfully configured functional conversion tracking. The company can now track marketing conversions across channels automatically and decisions can be supported with clearer data visibility. These insights also support revenue operations discussions, as understanding which marketing strategies perform best enables more informed resource allocation and strategic planning.
